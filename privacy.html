Privacy Policy

AstranoV — last updated 2026-05-25

This page describes how AstranoV processes personal data. We are a European operation; this policy is written to satisfy the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and equivalent regimes elsewhere.

1. Data controller

Notis Astranov, individual data controller. Contact: notisastranov@gmail.com. Postal address available on request for verified data-subject requests.

2. What we process

  • Account data: email, display name, optional avatar URL. Lawful basis: contract (Art. 6(1)(b)) — needed to provide the service to you.
  • Activity data: signals you post, comments, reactions, follows, deliveries you request or fulfil. Lawful basis: contract.
  • Location data: only when you explicitly tap "Locate" or grant geolocation permission. Lawful basis: consent (Art. 6(1)(a)).
  • Payment data: payments are processed by Stripe, Revolut, or PayPal (independent controllers). We see only the payment status, amount, currency, and the provider's order id; we never receive card numbers. Lawful basis: contract.
  • Tamper / security telemetry: when the Anonymous Service detects copy / save / devtools events, we log the event kind, IP, user-agent, language, screen resolution, and a 4-character preview of any copied text. Lawful basis: legitimate interest (Art. 6(1)(f)) — protecting our intellectual property and preventing abuse.
  • Camera snapshots: only after the user explicitly accepts the anti-fraud camera-verification prompt. Browsers cannot capture the camera silently. Lawful basis: explicit consent.
  • Compute contributions: if you opt in to donate cycles, we record the cycles donated, duration, and your user agent. Lawful basis: consent (opt-in).

3. What we do NOT process

  • Your filesystem, installed apps, or other apps' data. A browser cannot reach those even if asked.
  • Your card / bank details. Those go directly to Stripe / Revolut / PayPal under their own privacy policies.
  • Your private memories (those flagged is_private = true in our database) never reach any AI engine.

4. Third parties (processors)

The following sub-processors run parts of the system on our behalf. Each has a public privacy policy you should review:

  • Supabase Inc. — auth, database, edge functions, storage.
  • Vercel Inc. — static hosting + CDN.
  • Stripe Payments Europe, Revolut Ltd., PayPal (Europe) S.à r.l. — payment processing (independent controllers).
  • Anthropic PBC, OpenRouter, Groq Inc., Google (Gemini) — AI inference for the Astranov assistant, the Anonymous Service, and the developer channel. Public, non-private messages may be sent for inference.
  • NASA EOSDIS, OpenStreetMap, CARTO, Esri — map / imagery tiles (no personal data sent).
  • BBC, The Guardian, Al Jazeera, Deutsche Welle — public RSS feeds (no personal data sent).

5. Retention

  • Account data: while your account exists; deleted within 30 days of account deletion.
  • Signals + comments: retained until deleted by the author or the soft-delete policy purges them.
  • Tamper telemetry: 12 months, then automatic purge.
  • Payment records: 7 years (legal accounting obligation).
  • Compute contributions: while the AVC ledger needs them for payout reconciliation; anonymised aggregates may be kept indefinitely.

6. International transfers

Some processors operate outside the EEA (e.g. Anthropic in the US). Transfers happen under Standard Contractual Clauses or each processor's adequacy certification.

7. Your rights (GDPR Art. 15-22)

  • Access — request a copy of the data we hold about you.
  • Rectification — correct inaccurate data.
  • Erasure — delete your account and associated data.
  • Portability — receive your data in a machine-readable format.
  • Restriction / objection — limit or object to processing based on legitimate interest.
  • Withdraw consent — for location, camera, compute donation, and AI memory storage.

To exercise any right, email notisastranov@gmail.com. Requests honoured within 30 days.

8. Cookies / local storage

AstranoV uses localStorage for strictly-necessary functional state: your auth session, language, brightness, voice volume, depth focus, informants list, and (only if you opt in) your compute-donation flag. No tracking cookies. No third-party analytics.

9. Krypteia + the Anonymous Service

The Anonymous Service (visible to all users) analyses your browser fingerprint to advise you on your own device's privacy. It serves justful use: it will not assist actions that harm others. Owner-only deeper tools (Krypteia) operate under perpetual authorization granted by the data controller; they handle IP defence + audit telemetry only.

10. Complaints

If we have not resolved a concern to your satisfaction, you may lodge a complaint with your local data protection authority. For users in Greece, the supervisory authority is the Hellenic Data Protection Authority (dpa.gr).
